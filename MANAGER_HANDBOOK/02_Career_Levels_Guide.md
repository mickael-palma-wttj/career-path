## ğŸ”„ Understanding Career Transitions

### The Typical Progression Pattern

#### Early Career (Junior â†’ Confirmed â†’ Senior)
- **Growth Rate:** Relatively fast (1-3 years per level)
- **Focus:** Building skills, expanding capability
- **Measurement:** Can you do more complex work?
- **Blockers:** Usually skill/experience gaps

#### Mid-Career (Senior â†’ Staff)
- **Growth Rate:** Slower (3-5 years)
- **Focus:** Expanding scope and influence
- **Measurement:** Are you multiplying impact through others?
- **Blockers:** Often organizational constraints or scope

#### Late Career (Staff â†’ Senior Staff â†’ Principal)
- **Growth Rate:** Very slow or plateau (4+ years)
- **Focus:** Organizational and industry impact
- **Measurement:** Are you changing how the org/industry operates?
- **Blockers:** Often organizational size, opportunities, or personal choice

## ğŸ“ Calibration: How to Know What Level Someone Is

### Evidence-Based Assessment

**Ask yourself:**

1. **Scope:** What's the breadth and depth of their impact?
2. **Autonomy:** How much guidance do they need?
3. **Complexity:** What level of problem do they handle?
4. **Influence:** Who do they impact, and how?
5. **Consistency:** How reliable is their performance?

### Red Flags for Over-Leveling
- Requires frequent guidance for level-appropriate work
- Impact is limited to immediate tasks
- Struggles with ambiguity expected at that level
- Doesn't demonstrate expected leadership/influence
- Inconsistent performance

### Green Flags for Promotion Readiness
- Already performing at next level for 6+ months
- Multiple examples across different contexts
- Peer and stakeholder confirmation
- Consistent pattern, not one-off successes
- Demonstrated growth in development areas

## ğŸ¯ Manager's Role at Each Level

### Junior Developer
- **Your role:** Teacher and guide
- **Time investment:** High touch, frequent check-ins
- **Focus:** Building skills, confidence, and habits
- **Success:** They grow more independent each quarter

- **Time investment:** Moderate, regular career conversations
- **Focus:** Expanding capability and influence
- **Success:** They deliver reliably and help others

- **Your role:** Partner and sponsor
- **Time investment:** Lower, strategic guidance
- **Focus:** Broadening impact, developing leadership
- **Success:** They multiply team capability

- **Your role:** Collaborator and advocate
- **Time investment:** Minimal direct management
- **Focus:** Enabling organizational impact
- **Success:** They shape organizational direction
