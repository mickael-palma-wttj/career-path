# Common Scenarios

Quick reference guide for frequently encountered management situations with ICs.

---

## How to Use This Guide

Each scenario includes:
- **Situation:** What you're observing
- **Root causes:** Why this might be happening
- **Approaches:** 2-4 recommended responses
- **Red flags:** Warning signs
- **Related resources:** Where to learn more

**Remember:** Every person is different. Adapt these approaches to your specific situation.

## Scenario 1: IC Feels Stuck

### Situation
IC has been at same level for extended period and feels they're not progressing.

### Common Causes
- **Actually stuck:** Not performing at next level
- **Unclear expectations:** Doesn't know what next level requires
- **No opportunities:** Team too small or work not complex enough
- **Genuine plateau:** At right level for their capabilities/interest

### Approaches

**Approach 1: Diagnose the Gap**

1. **Assessment conversation:**
   "Let's look at the framework together. Where do you think you are on these criteria?"

2. **Compare to next level:**
   "Here's what Staff level requires. Here's where I see you currently. The gap is [specific areas]."

3. **Create development plan:**
   "Here's what we need to focus on over next 6-12 months..."

**When to use:** IC wants to progress but unclear on path.

**Approach 2: Create Opportunities**

1. **Identify growth projects:**
   "To demonstrate Staff-level work, you need to lead cross-team initiatives. Let's create that opportunity."

2. **Expand scope:**
   "I'm going to give you ownership of [larger area] where you can show next-level capability."

3. **Connect cross-team:**
   "I'll introduce you to [other team] for collaboration opportunity."

**When to use:** IC has capability but lacks opportunity to demonstrate it.

**Approach 3: Reality Check**

1. **Be honest about timeline:**
   "Based on framework, you're 12-18 months from ready. That's normal, not stuck."

2. **Or about plateau:**
   "You're a strong Senior. Not everyone progresses to Staff, and that's okay. Let's talk about keeping your work interesting and challenging."

3. **Or about constraints:**
   "Our team doesn't have Staff-level scope. To progress, you'd need to transfer to [larger team]. Want to explore that?"

**When to use:** Expectations need calibration.

## Scenario 2: Mismatch Between Self-Assessment and Reality

### Situation
IC rates themselves significantly higher than your assessment (e.g., they see 4.0, you see 2.5).

### Common Causes
- **Lack of awareness:** Dunning-Kruger effect
- **Different standards:** Comparing to wrong peers
- **Visibility gap:** You're not seeing their work
- **Defensive reaction:** Feels undervalued

### Approaches

**Approach 1: Evidence-Based Discussion**

1. **Ask for their reasoning:**
   "You've rated yourself 4.0. Walk me through what led you to that rating."

2. **Share specific examples:**
   "Here's what I've observed: [concrete behaviors]. Based on framework, that's ~2.5."

3. **Use framework as neutral reference:**
   "Let's look at level 4.0 description together. Does this match what you're doing?"

**When to use:** Most situationsâ€”leads to shared understanding.

**Approach 2: Investigate Blind Spots**

1. **Check if you're missing their work:**
   "Tell me about work you've done that I might not be aware of."

2. **Collect peer feedback:**
   "Let's get input from people you work with to triangulate."

3. **Adjust if warranted:**
   "You're right, I wasn't seeing [X]. That changes my assessment."

**When to use:** You suspect you may not have full picture.

**Approach 3: Show Comparison**

1. **Point to examples:**
   "Sarah is at 4.0. Here's what her work looks like. How does yours compare?"

2. **Highlight the gap:**
   "The difference is [specific capabilities]. That's the gap we need to close."

**When to use:** IC needs clearer mental model of levels.

ðŸš© Getting defensive or frustrated
ðŸš© Avoiding the hard conversation

## Scenario 3: High Performer Wanting Management Track

### Situation
Strong IC expressing interest in becoming a manager.

### Common Causes
- **Genuine interest:** Actually wants to manage people
- **Running away:** Feels stuck as IC
- **Status seeking:** Thinks management = advancement
- **Misunderstanding:** Doesn't know what management entails

### Approaches

**Approach 1: Explore Motivations**

1. **Discovery questions:**
   - "What appeals to you about management?"
   - "What do you think managers actually do day-to-day?"
   - "What concerns do you have?"

2. **Reality check:**
   "Management means less coding, more meetings, people problems, ambiguity. Still interested?"

3. **Test the waters:**
   "Let's have you mentor 2 juniors for 3 months and see how it feels."

**When to use:** Early exploration phase.

**Approach 2: Show IC Leadership Path**

1. **Explain Staff+ track:**
   "You can have significant impact and leadership as Staff+ IC. Here's what that looks like..."

2. **Show both paths:**
   "Let's explore both management and Staff IC paths in parallel for next 6 months."

3. **Let them decide:**
   "After trying mentorship and leading technical initiatives, which energizes you more?"

**When to use:** Want to ensure they know alternatives.

**Approach 3: Create Management Opportunity**

1. **If right fit:**
   "I think you'd be good at this. Here's path: 6 months of mentorship, then we can discuss formal role."

2. **Start small:**
   "Lead this small project team (2-3 people) for next quarter."

3. **Get training:**
   "Take management fundamentals course and shadow me in 1:1s."

**When to use:** They show genuine aptitude and interest.

### Red Flags
ðŸš© Immediately saying yes without exploring
ðŸš© Not being honest about what management is like
ðŸš© Pushing someone into management to keep them

## Scenario 4: Underperformance Issues

### Situation
IC consistently not meeting expectations for their level.

### Common Causes
- **Skill gaps:** Missing critical capabilities
- **Unclear expectations:** Doesn't know what's expected
- **Personal issues:** Outside factors affecting work
- **Wrong fit:** Role or level mismatch
- **Low motivation:** Checked out or unhappy

### Approaches

**Approach 1: Early Intervention**

1. **Direct feedback immediately:**
   "I'm seeing concerns with [specific behavior]. This needs to improve."

2. **Clarify expectations:**
   "Here's specifically what I need to see: [concrete expectations]."

3. **Offer support:**
   "What do you need from me to be successful?"

4. **Set timeline:**
   "Let's check back in 2 weeks to see progress."

**When to use:** First signs of underperformance.

**Approach 2: Formal Coaching**

1. **Document issues:**
   Write down specific examples and impact.

2. **Create improvement plan:**
   "Here's what needs to change by [date]. Here's how we'll measure success."

3. **Weekly check-ins:**
   Monitor progress closely and provide frequent feedback.

4. **Escalate if no improvement:**
   "We've discussed this for 6 weeks without change. This is now serious."

**When to use:** Early intervention didn't work.

**Approach 3: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**

1. **Involve HR:**
   Partner with HR on formal PIP.

2. **Clear terms:**
   "You have 60 days to demonstrate [specific improvements]. If not, we'll discuss your future here."

3. **Support but firm:**
   Provide resources but be clear about consequences.

**When to use:** Formal performance issues requiring documentation.

### Red Flags
ðŸš© Ignoring problems hoping they'll fix themselves
ðŸš© Vague feedback without specifics
ðŸš© Surprise termination without warning

## Scenario 5: Conflict with Peers

### Situation
IC having interpersonal conflicts with teammates or cross-team colleagues.

### Common Causes
- **Communication styles:** Mismatched approaches
- **Work style differences:** Collaboration preferences
- **Personality clash:** Just don't get along
- **Underlying issue:** Competing for resources, disagreement on direction
- **Behavioral problems:** One person being difficult

### Approaches

**Approach 1: Facilitate Resolution**

1. **Listen to both sides separately:**
   Understand each perspective without judgment.

2. **Identify core issue:**
   Is it communication, work process, values, or personality?

3. **Bring together:**
   "Let's find a way to work together effectively. What do each of you need?"

4. **Create agreement:**
   "Here's how you'll communicate and collaborate going forward."

**When to use:** Good-faith conflict that can be resolved.

**Approach 2: Coaching and Boundaries**

1. **Coach your IC:**
   "Here's how you could approach this differently..."

2. **Set expectations:**
   "Even if you disagree, you need to collaborate professionally."

3. **Monitor:**
   Check in regularly to ensure improvement.

**When to use:** Your IC is contributing to the problem.

**Approach 3: Escalate or Separate**

1. **Involve other manager:**
   Work with peer's manager if cross-team.

2. **Involve HR:**
   If harassment or serious behavior issues.

3. **Separate if needed:**
   Change team structure or project assignments.

**When to use:** Conflict is severe or not resolving.

### Red Flags
ðŸš© Taking sides without full picture
ðŸš© Ignoring the conflict
ðŸš© Allowing toxic behavior to continue

## Scenario 6: Rapid Growth Support

### Situation
IC progressing faster than typical timeline and needs accelerated development.

### Common Causes
- **High potential:** Exceptionally capable
- **Prior experience:** Comes with relevant background
- **Rapid learning:** Quick skill acquisition
- **High motivation:** Extremely driven

### Approaches

**Approach 1: Accelerate Strategically**

1. **Assess readiness honestly:**
   "You're moving fast. Let's make sure foundation is solid before jumping ahead."

2. **Create stretch opportunities:**
   Give increasingly challenging projects.

3. **Expand scope:**
   Multi-team leadership, strategic initiatives.

4. **Don't skip levels:**
   Fast progression through levels, but don't skip.

**When to use:** IC demonstrating genuine next-level capability.

**Approach 2: Provide Advanced Support**

1. **Connect with senior mentors:**
   Pair with Staff+ engineers for guidance.

2. **Expose to leadership:**
   Visibility with senior leadership for sponsorship.

3. **Advanced learning:**
   Conferences, executive coaching, specialized training.

**When to use:** Developing future leader.

**Approach 3: Watch for Risks**

1. **Monitor for gaps:**
   Ensure no critical skills being skipped.

2. **Prevent burnout:**
   Balance rapid growth with sustainability.

3. **Manage expectations:**
   "You're progressing fast. Next level takes longerâ€”stay patient."

**When to use:** Alwaysâ€”balance acceleration with sustainability.

## General Principles

### Act Early
- Don't wait for situations to become crises
- Address issues when they're small
- Provide feedback in real-time

### Be Direct and Kind
- Clarity is kindness
- Specific feedback, not vague
- Honest about reality

### Use the Framework
- Reference evaluation criteria
- Make objective, not subjective
- Create shared language

### Document Everything
- Keep notes on conversations
- Track patterns and progress
- Protect yourself and IC

### Get Support
- Consult your manager
- Partner with HR when appropriate
- Learn from peers
