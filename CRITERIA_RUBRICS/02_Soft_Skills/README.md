# Soft Skills

Interpersonal capabilities that enable collaboration, growth, and effectiveness beyond individual technical work.

---

## üìã What's in This Section

This section covers the 5 core soft skills criteria that assess people's ability to work effectively with others and drive themselves:

### Criteria

1. **[Empathy and Humility](01_Empathy_and_Humility.md)**
   - Perspective-taking, feedback openness, growth mindset
   - Understanding others' viewpoints and experiences
   - Learning from mistakes and setbacks

2. **[Communication](02_Communication.md)**
   - Clarity, listening, audience adaptation
   - Expressing ideas and understanding others
   - Tailoring communication to context

3. **[Initiative](03_Initiative.md)**
   - Proactive problem-solving, self-direction, ownership
   - Spotting issues without being asked
   - Taking responsibility for outcomes

4. **[Objectivity and Adaptability](04_Objectivity_and_Adaptability.md)**
   - Data-driven thinking, adaptability, resilience
   - Making decisions based on evidence
   - Handling change and pressure

5. **[Business Acumen](05_Business_Acumen.md)**
   - Customer focus, ROI thinking, strategic alignment
   - Understanding business context
   - Thinking beyond technical implementation

## üéØ How to Use This Section

### For Assessment
1. Review the specific criterion rubric
2. Look at the 5 levels and evidence to look for
3. Ask the assessment questions in meetings and 1:1s
4. Observe behaviors over time, not just one instance
5. Rate based on consistent patterns

### For Development
1. Identify current level with IC
2. Review specific gaps between current and target level
3. Use coaching strategies appropriate to their level
4. Provide real examples and feedback
5. Set development goals with accountability

### For Calibration
1. Reference the rating scale (0.0-1.0, 1.0-2.0, etc.)
2. Use consistent evidence standards across team
3. Discuss edge cases with management peers
4. Align on what each level "looks like"
5. Consider context (remote, new to team, etc.)

| Level | Range | Expectation |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Developing | 0.0-1.0 | Learning, reactive, needs coaching |
| Competent | 1.0-2.0 | Generally good, sometimes struggles |
| Proficient | 2.0-3.0 | Consistent, reliable, independent |
| Expert | 3.0-3.5 | Exemplary, mentors others |
| Authority | 3.5-4.0 | Org-wide model, shapes culture |

## üí° Key Insights

### Soft Skills Across Levels

**Developing Engineers:**
- Figuring out how to work with others
- Still learning professional norms
- Need direct, kind feedback
- Building foundational habits

**Competent Engineers:**
- Generally collaborative and professional
- Sometimes defensive or reactive
- Learning from feedback
- Improving consistency

**Proficient Engineers:**
- Strong interpersonal baseline
- Can handle difficult situations
- Support others' growth
- Contributing to team culture

**Expert Engineers:**
- Model soft skills consistently
- Help others develop these skills
- Navigate complex situations with grace
- Shape positive team dynamics

**Authority Engineers:**
- Exemplars of soft skills
- Influence org culture
- Mentor leaders on these skills
- Set the standard

## ‚ùì Quick Questions by Criterion

**Empathy & Humility:** Tell me about a time someone gave you critical feedback. How did you respond?

**Communication:** Describe a complex idea you recently explained. How did you make sure people understood?

**Initiative:** What problem have you solved on your own initiative? How did you spot it?

**Objectivity:** When you disagreed with a decision, how did you handle it? What data informed your perspective?

**Business Acumen:** How do you think about what we're building from a business perspective?

- **Feedback:** Regular, specific feedback on interpersonal impact
- **Mentoring:** Working with someone strong in this skill area
- **Self-Reflection:** Regular reflection on own reactions and patterns
- **Communication Training:** Formal or informal coaching
- **Diverse Interactions:** Working with people different from yourself
- **High-Stakes Projects:** Learning under pressure and complexity

## üîÑ Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: "They're technically strong but interpersonally weak"
**Solution:** 
- Make soft skills expectations explicit
- Provide specific feedback and coaching
- Pair with strong soft skills mentors
- Set behavioral goals with accountability
- Recognize improvement efforts

### Challenge: "They're great people but average technical skills"
**Solution:**
- Ensure technical development is prioritized equally
- Pair technical skills development with soft skills strength
- Use their interpersonal strength to learn from others
- Set technical growth goals
- Provide technical mentoring and stretch projects

### Challenge: "Mixed signals - different people see them differently"
**Solution:**
- Gather more evidence before rating
- Investigate context (who do they struggle with, when?)
- Look for patterns, not exceptions
- Discuss with others who interact with them
- Be specific in feedback about situations
