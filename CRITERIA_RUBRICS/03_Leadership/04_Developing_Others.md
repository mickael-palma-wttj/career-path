# Developing Others

**Criterion:** Coaching, mentoring, and growing team members' skills and careers to accelerate their development and create next generation of leaders.

This evaluates your ability to see potential in people, create growth opportunities, provide effective coaching and mentoring, and intentionally develop careers. Strong developers are multipliers‚Äîthey build capability that compounds throughout the organization.

## üéØ Definition

**Developing Others** encompasses five interconnected capabilities:

### 1. **Identifying Potential**
Seeing capability and growth potential in people. Not just evaluating current performance, but understanding who has potential to grow, what they're capable of becoming, and where they're ready to stretch.

### 2. **Coaching & Teaching**
Providing effective coaching that helps people improve. This includes teaching technical skills, providing feedback, asking powerful questions, and creating learning opportunities. Good coaches accelerate learning.

### 3. **Career Planning & Guidance**
Helping people think strategically about their career. Understanding their aspirations, what skills they need, creating path forward, and helping them see how to get there.

### 4. **Creating Growth Opportunities**
Providing stretch projects, assignments, and challenges that develop people. Growth happens through experience with support, not in comfort zone.

### 5. **Accountability & Follow-up**
Following through on development. Checking in, providing feedback, adjusting approaches, celebrating progress. Development is ongoing, not one-time event.

## üìö Detailed Development Guidance

### Identifying Potential

**What to Look For:**
- **Learning agility** - Quickly learns new skills; bounces back from failure
- **Growth mindset** - Sees challenges as opportunities; wants to grow
- **Curiosity** - Asks questions; wants to understand deeply
- **Resilience** - Persists through difficulty; doesn't give up
- **Self-awareness** - Knows strengths and gaps; open to feedback
- **Initiative** - Takes ownership; doesn't wait to be told
- **Impact** - Creates value; makes things happen

**Common Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Only seeing potential in people like you
- ‚ùå Mistaking intelligence for potential (smart ‚â† grows)
- ‚ùå Missing potential in quiet people
- ‚ùå Assuming current performance = max potential
- ‚ùå Not seeing potential in people who struggle early

**How to Identify:**
- Ask: "How do they respond to feedback?"
- Ask: "Do they own their development?"
- Ask: "Have they surprised you by growing?"
- Ask: "Would you bet on them?"

### Effective Coaching

**Coaching Formula:**
1. **Observe** - See what's happening; understand situation
2. **Ask** - Ask questions rather than tell; help them think through
3. **Listen** - Really hear what they're saying; understand their perspective
4. **Reflect** - Mirror back what you're hearing
5. **Suggest** - Offer perspective or options
6. **Support** - Be available as they implement
7. **Check back** - See how it went; learn together

**Types of Coaching:**
- **Skill coaching** - "Here's how to do this technical thing"
- **Performance coaching** - "Here's how to improve your delivery"
- **Career coaching** - "Here's how to think about your path"
- **Behavioral coaching** - "Here's how to be more effective with people"

**Common Coaching Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Telling instead of asking (takes away their learning)
- ‚ùå Coaching only when there's a problem
- ‚ùå Same approach for everyone (one-size-fits-all)
- ‚ùå Not following up
- ‚ùå Coaching without understanding their perspective first

### Creating Growth Opportunities

**Stretch Assignment Approach:**
1. **Identify growth area** - What do they need to develop?
2. **Find assignment** - What project/challenge builds that?
3. **Right difficulty** - Hard enough to grow, possible to succeed
4. **Provide support** - Be available; don't set them up to fail
5. **Check in** - How are they doing? What are they learning?
6. **Reflect** - What did they learn? How did they grow?

**Types of Opportunities:**
- **New project** - Something they haven't done
- **Leadership role** - Leading part of project or person
- **Mentoring** - Teaching someone else (great development)
- **Presentation** - Sharing work publicly
- **Cross-org project** - Learning how other parts work
- **Problem-solving** - Addressing a real org problem

**Common Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Too hard; they fail and lose confidence
- ‚ùå Not hard enough; doesn't develop them
- ‚ùå No support; they drown
- ‚ùå No reflection; they don't internalize learning

### Career Planning

**Career Conversation Framework:**
1. **Understand aspirations** - Where do they want to go?
2. **Assess current state** - What are they good at? What gaps?
3. **Identify path** - What do they need to develop?
4. **Create plan** - Specific actions, learning, projects
5. **Support execution** - Coaching, opportunities, feedback
6. **Review progress** - Check in regularly; adjust as needed

**Career Paths to Discuss:**
- **IC progression** - Junior ‚Üí Confirmed ‚Üí Senior ‚Üí Staff ‚Üí Senior Staff ‚Üí Principal
- **Manager progression** - IC ‚Üí Manager ‚Üí Senior Manager ‚Üí Director
- **Specialist** - Deep expertise in one area
- **Generalist** - Broad expertise across areas
- **Lateral** - Moving to learn new area

**Common Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Only discussing promotions, not development
- ‚ùå Assuming everyone wants to be manager
- ‚ùå Not having career conversations regularly
- ‚ùå Setting path without understanding aspirations

### Follow-up & Accountability

**How to Follow Through:**
- Regular check-ins (monthly or quarterly)
- Update development plans quarterly
- Celebrate progress and wins
- Adjust approach based on what's working
- Provide feedback on development
- Remove blockers to learning

**Tracking Development:**
- What skills improved?
- Did they succeed in stretch assignment?
- Are they progressing toward goals?
- What's next?
- Are they growing?

## üí° Key Principles

### 1. **Development Is Your Highest Leverage Work**
One great developer multiplies impact through the people they develop. This is how leaders compound their impact.

### 2. **Everyone Can Develop**
Don't assume people are fixed. With right coaching and opportunities, people grow. Growth mindset matters.

### 3. **Development Takes Time**
Real development doesn't happen in one conversation. It's ongoing coaching, feedback, opportunities, reflection over time.

### 4. **Support While Stretching**
Stretch people but support them. Don't throw them in sink-or-swim. Hard but possible with support.

### 5. **Development Is Personal**
Different people develop differently. Tailor your coaching to the individual. One approach doesn't work for everyone.

### 6. **Follow Through**
Development requires ongoing follow-up. Don't just have one conversation and expect growth. Check in, provide feedback, adjust.
