# Team Leadership

**Criterion:** Setting direction, making decisions, building team cohesion, and leading by example to create high-performing, aligned teams.

This evaluates your ability to provide vision and direction, make sound decisions that the team respects, build psychological safety and cohesion, and model the behaviors you expect from your team. Strong team leadership creates teams that know where they're going and trust the person leading them.

## üéØ Definition

**Team Leadership** encompasses five interconnected capabilities:

### 1. **Vision & Direction**
Setting clear, compelling direction that gives the team purpose and focus. Vision explains both where the team is going and why it matters. It provides context for decisions and helps the team make tradeoffs.

### 2. **Decision-Making**
Making sound decisions that the team respects. Good leadership decisions are: timely (not delayed), well-reasoned, considerate of team input, and explainable. Leaders make decisions others would have made if they had the full picture.

### 3. **Team Cohesion**
Building psychological safety and unity. Teams need to trust each other, feel safe being vulnerable, disagree respectfully, and rally around shared purpose. Cohesion is built through consistency, fairness, and shared experience.

### 4. **Trust & Respect**
Earning the team's trust through competence, reliability, and integrity. Trust is earned through: doing what you say you'll do, having their back, being fair, admitting mistakes, and making decisions in their best interest.

### 5. **Leading by Example**
Modeling the behaviors and attitudes you expect from the team. If you want excellence, you demonstrate it. If you want accountability, you're accountable. If you want vulnerability, you're vulnerable first.

## üìä Detailed Level Descriptions

### üü¶ Developing (0.0 - 1.0)

**Core Behaviors:**
- Doesn't set clear direction; team is unclear on priorities or purpose
- Avoids making decisions; delegates without clarity or changes decisions frequently
- Team is fragmented; lacks cohesion and shared purpose
- Team doesn't trust their judgment; decisions seem arbitrary or inconsistent
- Doesn't model leadership behaviors; may model negative behaviors
- Focuses on tasks rather than team development
- Leadership style is passive or reactive

**What You'd See:**
- Team meetings where there's confusion about priorities
- "I'm not sure what the leader wants from us"
- Decisions change frequently without explanation
- Team members work in silos; little collaboration
- High turnover or frequent complaints
- Leader avoids difficult conversations
- Team doesn't defend their leader; indifferent about them
- Work is delivered but no sense of purpose

**What Success Looks Like:**
- Articulates clear priority for team
- Makes a decision and explains reasoning
- Engages team in discussing direction
- Follows through on a commitment to team
- Acknowledges mistake and adjusts

**Development Focus:**
- Build self-awareness about leadership impact
- Learn to set direction and priorities
- Practice decision-making with coaching
- Understand what team needs from leader
- Develop authenticity and vulnerability

### üü© Competent (1.0 - 2.0)

**Core Behaviors:**
- Sets some direction, though it may shift; team has basic understanding of priorities
- Makes decisions that are mostly sound; some lack clarity or reasoning
- Team has moderate cohesion; some collaboration
- Team respects their judgment most of the time; some questions emerge
- Attempts to model leadership behaviors; inconsistent
- Focuses on task delivery; some attention to team development
- Leadership is functional but not inspiring

**What You'd See:**
- Team generally understands direction, though it may not be crystal clear
- "They usually make good calls" (though sometimes questions why)
- Decisions are reasonably consistent; team can count on that
- Some team collaboration; mostly organized by task
- Moderate turnover; some people want to stay
- Some difficult conversations happen; others are avoided
- Team respects leader but wouldn't describe them as inspiring
- Work is delivered on time with adequate quality

**Specific Examples:**
- "Our leader said this quarter we're focused on reliability. I know what that means for my work."
- "When they made the decision to delay the launch, they explained their thinking. I didn't agree but understood."
- "They admit when they're wrong. That makes me more willing to take risks."

**What Success Looks Like:**
- Set quarterly direction that team aligns around
- Make difficult decision and explain it to team
- Team describes you as fair and thoughtful
- Show up consistently for team
- Admit a mistake and adjust

**Development Focus:**
- Develop clearer, more compelling vision
- Improve decision-making clarity and timing
- Build stronger team relationships
- Increase consistency and predictability
- Model leadership more authentically

## ‚ùì Assessment Questions

### For Any Level:
- **What's the direction you're setting for your team?** How did you decide on it? How did you communicate it?
- **Tell me about a recent decision you made as a leader.** How did you make it? Did you involve your team? How did they respond?
- **How would your team describe working for you?** What would they say are your strengths and weaknesses?

### For Competent+ Levels:
- **Describe your team's cohesion.** How do they work together? Do they help each other?
- **Tell me about a time you had to make an unpopular decision.** How did you handle it?
- **How do you build trust with your team?** What does that look like?

### For Proficient+ Levels:
- **Walk me through your approach to developing your team members.** How do you think about growth?
- **Tell me about someone from your team who was promoted.** How did you develop them?
- **Describe how you handle conflict or disagreement on your team.** Give me a specific example.
- **What's your leadership philosophy?** How does it guide your decisions?

### For Expert+ Levels:
- **Tell me about the culture you've created on your team.** How did you create it? What makes it special?
- **How have you shaped your team members into leaders?** Tell me about multiple examples.**
- **What's your biggest impact as a leader?** How do you know it's working?
- **How do you mentor other leaders on team leadership?**

## üìö Detailed Development Guidance

### Setting Clear Direction

**Common Mistakes:**
- **Too vague** - Vision doesn't actually guide decisions ("Do good work")
- **Too detailed** - Vision is task list, not direction ("Complete X, Y, Z")
- **Too disconnected from business** - Team doesn't understand why it matters
- **Doesn't change when needed** - Stuck to old vision even as circumstances change
- **Changes too frequently** - Team confused because direction keeps shifting

**How to Set Good Direction:**
1. **Understand context** - What's happening in business? What does org need?
2. **Think about 6-12 months** - Where do you want team to be?
3. **Make it concrete but not a task list** - "Become the reliability experts the org depends on" not "Fix these 5 bugs"
4. **Connect to why** - Why does this matter? What's the business impact?
5. **Make it memorable** - Can team remember and repeat it?
6. **Get feedback** - Does it make sense to team? Does it excite them?
7. **Review quarterly** - Is it still right? Does it need adjusting?

### Making Good Decisions

**Decision-Making Process:**
1. **Clarify the decision** - What exactly are we deciding?
2. **Get input** - Who needs to weigh in? What do they know?
3. **Consider options** - What are the tradeoffs?
4. **Decide** - Make a clear call, even with incomplete information
5. **Explain** - Why this decision? What did you consider?
6. **Implement** - Be clear on next steps
7. **Check back** - Did it work? What did we learn?

**Common Decision Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Too slow - Wait for perfect information (never comes)
- ‚ùå Without input - Decide in vacuum without hearing team
- ‚ùå Without explanation - Team left wondering why
- ‚ùå Change without reason - Flip-flop undermines trust
- ‚ùå Pretend it was obvious - Acknowledge difficulty if it was hard
- ‚ùå No accountability - Don't learn from outcome

### Building Psychological Safety

**Psychological safety** is feeling safe to take risks, be vulnerable, ask questions, and admit mistakes without fear of embarrassment or punishment.

**How to Build It:**
1. **Be vulnerable first** - Admit when you don't know something or make a mistake
2. **Respond well to mistakes** - When someone fails, focus on learning not blame
3. **Invite input** - Ask questions; show you want to hear different perspectives
4. **Respond to input** - Don't ask for input then ignore it
5. **Address conflicts directly** - Don't let tensions fester
6. **Be consistent** - Treat people fairly; don't play favorites
7. **Protect the team** - Have their back to leadership

**How You Know You Have It:**
- Team disagrees in meetings without fear
- People admit mistakes quickly
- Questions and "I don't know" are common
- People take on stretch assignments
- Conflicts are addressed directly
- Retention is high

### Leading by Example

**What this means:**
- If you want excellence, demonstrate it
- If you want accountability, be accountable
- If you want transparency, be transparent
- If you want learning, show willingness to learn
- If you want collaboration, collaborate

**Common Failures:**
- ‚ùå Demand perfection but make mistakes without acknowledging
- ‚ùå Ask for honesty but punish bad news
- ‚ùå Want collaboration but make unilateral decisions
- ‚ùå Expect long hours but leave early yourself
- ‚ùå Ask for vulnerability but never show yours

### Developing Your Team Members

**Development Approach:**
1. **Understand their aspirations** - Where do they want to go?
2. **Identify gaps** - What do they need to develop?
3. **Create opportunities** - Stretch projects, mentoring, learning
4. **Provide support** - Don't just throw them in sink or swim
5. **Give feedback** - Help them see progress
6. **Celebrate growth** - Acknowledge when they level up

**Track What Works:**
- People promoted from your team?
- People who stayed and advanced?
- People who left for bigger roles (sometimes success)?
- Career trajectories of team members?

## üí° Key Principles

### 1. **Direction Matters**
Teams with clear direction perform better. Ambiguity creates stress. Clear direction creates focus. Spend time on this.

### 2. **Decisions Build Trust**
How you make decisions matters as much as what decision you make. Team trusts leaders who decide well: getting input, explaining reasoning, following through.

### 3. **Psychological Safety Enables Excellence**
Teams without safety play it safe. Safe teams take risks, try new things, admit mistakes, collaborate. This is where great work happens.

### 4. **Trust Is Foundation**
Without trust, everything else is hard. With trust, everything is possible. Build trust through consistency, fairness, follow-through, and vulnerability.

### 5. **Develop Your People**
The measure of a great leader is the people they develop. Don't just do the work; build the next generation.

### 6. **Lead by Example**
You can't ask for what you don't model. Your behavior sets the tone. If you want excellence, demonstrate it.
