## üéØ How to Use This Section

### For Assessment
1. Review the specific criterion rubric
2. Look at the 5 levels and evidence to look for
3. Ask assessment questions in appropriate contexts
4. Gather feedback from peers and reports
5. Observe impact on team and organization
6. Rate based on demonstrated behaviors

### For Development
1. Identify current level with IC
2. Review specific gaps between current and target level
3. Use coaching strategies appropriate to their level
4. Create developmental stretch opportunities
5. Set leadership growth goals with accountability

### For Calibration
1. Reference the rating scale (0.0-1.0, 1.0-2.0, etc.)
2. Use consistent evidence standards across team
3. Discuss edge cases with management peers
4. Align on what leadership "looks like" at each level
5. Consider organization and context

## üí° Key Insights

### Leadership Across Levels

**IC Track Leadership (Junior ‚Üí Senior):**
- Individual contributor focused
- Limited direct reports
- Leadership through influence, not authority
- Technical excellence + growing soft leadership

**Staff+ Track Leadership (Staff ‚Üí Principal):**
- Visible leadership across org
- May or may not have direct reports
- Influence through expertise and relationships
- Shaping org direction and culture

**Engineering Manager Track:**
- Direct responsibility for people
- Team performance accountability
- Leadership through authority and support
- Development of team members

### Leadership Without Management

**Important:** Leadership isn't limited to managers. Strong ICs demonstrate leadership through:
- Influence and vision-setting
- Developing peers and juniors
- Cross-org relationship building
- Shaping culture and values
- Mentoring and coaching

## üîÑ Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: "Strong technical skills but no leadership interest"
**Solution:**
- Not all ICs want or need strong leadership skills
- Can still rate proficient/expert in narrow leadership areas
- Focus development on what's needed for their path
- Respect different career goals

### Challenge: "Great leader but technical skills are slipping"
**Solution:**
- Balance leadership and technical expectations by level
- For Staff+ roles, can shift weight toward leadership
- Ensure technical credibility for advice-giving
- Decide on career direction clearly

### Challenge: "They have authority but people don't follow"
**Solution:**
- Explore why trust is lacking (competence? values? safety?)
- Gather feedback from team/stakeholders
- Address specific behaviors or gaps
- May need development before promotion

### Challenge: "Limited formal leadership opportunities"
**Solution:**
- Look for informal leadership opportunities
- Start with mentoring one junior
- Lead technical projects or initiatives
- Build relationships cross-functionally
- Coach in team meetings

## üéØ Leadership at Different Career Levels

### Junior ‚Üí Confirmed (Developing Stage)
- Starting to mentor someone
- Contributing to team culture
- Building peer relationships

### Confirmed ‚Üí Senior (Competent Stage)
- Clear mentoring impact
- Cross-team relationships
- Respected perspective on decisions

### Senior ‚Üí Staff (Proficient Stage)
- Strong relationships across org
- Visible mentoring of juniors
- Shaping technical culture
- Influencing org decisions

### Staff ‚Üí Senior Staff (Expert Stage)
- Org leadership presence
- Developing future leaders
- Culture shapers
- Strategic influence

### Senior Staff ‚Üí Principal (Authority Stage)
- Org leadership model
- Developing other leaders
- Setting company direction
- Industry influence
