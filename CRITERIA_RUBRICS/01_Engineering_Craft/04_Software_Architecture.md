## ğŸ¯ Definition

**Software Architecture** means:
- **System Design** - Structuring systems for scalability and maintainability
- **Tradeoff Awareness** - Understanding technical tradeoffs
- **Scalability Thinking** - Designing for growth
- **Long-term View** - Considering future needs and changes
- **Design Communication** - Explaining architectural choices

---

## ğŸ“Š Level-by-Level Breakdown

### Developing (0.0-1.0)
- Designs features in isolation
- Doesn't consider larger system
- No awareness of tradeoffs
- Treats code as temporary
- Asks for architecture guidance

### Competent (1.0-2.0)
- Designs within established patterns
- Considers immediate needs
- Follows architectural guidance from seniors
- Some awareness of scaling issues
- Learning design thinking

### Proficient (2.0-3.0)
- Designs thoughtful systems
- Considers scalability and maintainability
- Makes good tradeoff decisions
- Others reference their designs
- Contributes to architecture decisions

### Expert (3.0-3.5)
- Exceptional architectural sense
- Anticipates future needs
- Makes sophisticated design tradeoffs
- Mentors others on architecture
- Drives architectural improvements

### Authority (3.5-4.0)
- Org's architectural authority
- Sets architectural standards
- Influences org-wide design decisions
- Others follow their architectural approach
- Visible improvements in system architecture
