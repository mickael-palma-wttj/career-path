# Technical Understanding

**Criterion:** Deep knowledge of systems, technologies, frameworks, and the ability to learn and apply new technical concepts.

---

## üìã Quick Summary

| Level | Expectation |
|-------|-------------|
| **Developing (0.0-1.0)** | Learning fundamentals; needs guidance on technology questions |
| **Competent (1.0-2.0)** | Solid understanding of core systems; mostly independent |
| **Proficient (2.0-3.0)** | Deep knowledge of relevant systems; goes to person for questions |
| **Expert (3.0-3.5)** | Exceptional depth and breadth; sets technical direction |
| **Authority (3.5-4.0)** | Org authority on technical topics; shapes technical choices |

---

## üéØ Definition

**Technical Understanding** means having:
- **Depth** - Deep knowledge of systems you work with
- **Breadth** - Awareness of technologies beyond immediate domain
- **Fundamentals** - Strong grasp of underlying principles
- **Learning Ability** - Can pick up new technologies quickly
- **System Thinking** - Understand how pieces fit together

---

## üìä Level-by-Level Breakdown

### Level 0.0-1.0: Developing
**What You See:**
- Struggles with concepts; frequently asks why things work
- Doesn't understand system architecture
- Treats technology as black boxes
- Learns slowly; needs repeated explanations
- Can't troubleshoot beyond immediate problem
- Looks at similar code to copy patterns

**Manager's Focus:**
- Provide learning resources and patience
- Pair with more experienced people
- Explain WHY, not just HOW
- Build fundamentals, not just recipes
- Celebrate learning progress

---

### Level 1.0-2.0: Competent
**What You See:**
- Understands core systems reasonably well
- Can solve most routine problems independently
- Knows the major components and how they interact
- Learning faster but still needs guidance on complex areas
- Can follow documentation to solve new problems
- Understanding improves with each project

**Manager's Focus:**
- Expose to new technologies gradually
- Ask them to explain how things work
- Recommend learning resources
- Give problems that stretch understanding
- Build deeper expertise gradually

---

### Level 2.0-3.0: Proficient
**What You See:**
- Deep understanding of systems they work with
- Go-to person for questions about those systems
- Can explain why things are designed certain ways
- Understand tradeoffs and design choices
- Quickly picks up new related technologies
- Contributes to architecture discussions meaningfully

**Manager's Focus:**
- Ask them to document complex systems
- Use them as teachers for others
- Expose to strategic technical decisions
- Challenge with novel technical problems
- Support continuous learning

---

### Level 3.0-3.5: Expert
**What You See:**
- Exceptional technical depth and breadth
- Sets technical direction on multiple systems
- Can evaluate competing technical approaches
- Quickly masters new technologies
- Understands cutting edge in their domain
- Others ask them for technical guidance

**Manager's Focus:**
- Leverage for technical mentoring
- Involve in key technical decisions
- Support research and learning
- Challenge with strategic problems
- Recognize as technical authority

---

### Level 3.5-4.0: Authority
**What You See:**
- Organization's technical authority
- Shapes technical direction and strategy
- Others learn their approach to tech
- Drives adoption of new technologies
- Recognized internally and externally

**Manager's Focus:**
- Support leadership role
- Involve in org-wide tech decisions
- Amplify influence on technical direction
- Challenge with complex problems
- Enable thought leadership

## üéì Coaching Strategies

**For Developing:**
- Pair with experts; learning by observation
- Explain fundamentals, not just procedures
- Recommend focused learning resources
- Build confidence through small wins

**For Competent:**
- Gradually expand scope of learning
- Ask them to explain how things work
- Introduce new technologies to learn
- Support deeper learning

**For Proficient:**
- Position as teacher and expert
- Involve in technical decisions
- Recommend advanced learning
- Challenge with strategic tech questions

**For Expert/Authority:**
- Support thought leadership
- Involve in org-wide technical strategy
- Enable continuous growth
- Amplify influence

## üìù Real Examples

**Developing:** "I don't know why that's happening. Can you show me what to do?"

**Competent:** "I've seen this pattern before. Let me check the documentation to remember the right way to do it."

**Proficient:** "This design makes sense because it separates concerns this way. Let me explain why we chose this approach over the alternative."

**Expert:** "I'd approach this by first understanding the constraints, then evaluating these three technical approaches. Here's why I'd recommend this one."

**Authority:** "I've seen this pattern across the industry. Here's how we should approach it at our scale."
