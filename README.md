# IC Career Development Framework

A comprehensive handbook for managers to guide Individual Contributors (ICs) through their career progression from Junior Developer to Principal Developer.

---

## üìñ What is This?

This framework provides:
- **Clear career progression paths** across 6 levels
- **Detailed evaluation criteria** across 3 core pillars
- **Practical tools and templates** for managers
- **Evidence-based assessment methods**
- **Real-world guidance** for common scenarios

### The 6 Career Levels

1. [**Junior Developer**](CAREER_LEVEL_GUIDES/01_Junior_Developer.md) - Building foundations
2. [**Confirmed Developer**](CAREER_LEVEL_GUIDES/02_Confirmed_Developer.md) - Gaining confidence and autonomy
3. [**Senior Developer**](CAREER_LEVEL_GUIDES/03_Senior_Developer.md) - Leading by example and mentoring
4. [**Staff Developer**](CAREER_LEVEL_GUIDES/04_Staff_Developer.md) - Technical leadership across teams
5. [**Senior Staff Developer**](CAREER_LEVEL_GUIDES/05_Senior_Staff_Developer.md) - Organizational impact and strategy
6. [**Principal Developer**](CAREER_LEVEL_GUIDES/06_Principal_Developer.md) - Company-wide technical leadership

### The 3 Evaluation Pillars

**üîß Engineering Craft (40-65% weight)**
- Writing Code, Testing, Technical Understanding
- Software Architecture, Security, Debugging & Monitoring

**üí¨ Soft Skills (25-40% weight)**
- Empathy & Humility, Communication, Initiative
- Objectivity & Adaptability, Business Acumen

**üë• Leadership (5-20% weight)**
- Hiring & Org Design, Stakeholder Management
- Team Leadership, Developing Others, Culture & Togetherness

## üéØ How to Use This Handbook

### Monthly Career Development Cycle

**Week 1: Preparation**
- Review IC's current goals and progress
- Collect peer feedback if needed
- Prepare 1:1 agenda with career discussion

**Week 2-3: Active Coaching**
- Conduct 1:1 with career focus
- Provide specific feedback on observed behaviors
- Identify stretch opportunities

**Week 4: Planning & Documentation**
- Update development plan
- Track progress against goals
- Identify any blockers or support needed

### Quarterly Deep Dives

- **Q1**: Set annual goals, review previous year
- **Q2**: Mid-year check-in, adjust goals
- **Q3**: Start promotion conversations if relevant
- **Q4**: Year-end review, plan for next year

### Annual Activities

- Formal performance reviews
- Promotion evaluations and decisions
- Compensation discussions
- Long-term career planning (3-5 years)

## üß≠ Core Principles

### 1. **Growth is Everyone's Responsibility**
- Managers create opportunities and remove blockers
- ICs own their development and drive their careers
- Collaboration between manager and IC is essential

### 2. **Clarity Builds Trust**
- Be explicit about expectations at each level
- Provide regular, specific feedback
- Make evaluation criteria transparent

### 3. **Progress Over Perfection**
- No one is perfect at every criterion
- Growth is non-linear and sometimes slow
- Celebrate incremental improvements

### 4. **Evidence-Based Decisions**
- Collect concrete examples of behaviors
- Use multiple data sources (peers, stakeholders, projects)
- Document observations consistently

### 5. **Fair ‚â† Equal**
- Different ICs need different support
- Tailor your approach to individual needs
- Be consistent in standards, flexible in methods

## üìä Rating Scale

The framework uses a **0.0 to 4.0+ scale**:

| Rating | Meaning | Typical Level |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| 0.0 - 1.0 | **Developing** - Learning this skill, needs guidance | Junior |
| 1.0 - 2.0 | **Competent** - Reliable execution with some support | Confirmed |
| 2.0 - 3.0 | **Proficient** - Consistent, independent performance | Senior |
| 3.0 - 3.5 | **Expert** - Deep expertise, mentors others | Staff/Senior Staff |
| 3.5 - 4.0 | **Authority** - Organization-wide impact | Principal |

**Note:** Ratings are contextual and should be interpreted within the full criterion description.

## ‚ùì Common Questions

**How often should I discuss career progress with ICs?**
At minimum, dedicate one 1:1 per month to career development. More frequent for those actively working on promotion.

**What if someone disagrees with their assessment?**
Use the rubrics to point to specific behaviors and examples. Collect peer feedback. Be open to hearing their perspective and adjusting if new evidence emerges.

**Can someone skip a level?**
Rarely. Each level builds essential skills for the next. Exceptions might be made for external hires with proven experience.

**How long does promotion typically take?**
Varies widely by individual, but rough guidelines:
- Junior ‚Üí Confirmed: 12-24 months
- Confirmed ‚Üí Senior: 2-4 years
- Senior ‚Üí Staff: 3-5 years
- Staff ‚Üí Senior Staff: 3-5 years
- Beyond Senior Staff: 5+ years
