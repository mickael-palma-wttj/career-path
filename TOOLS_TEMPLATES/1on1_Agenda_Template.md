## üìã Template 1: Weekly Check-in

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes  
**Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly

### Pre-Meeting Prep (Manager)
- [ ] Review last 1:1 notes and action items
- [ ] Check recent work (PRs, projects, artifacts)
- [ ] Prepare any feedback from recent observations
- [ ] Note any team/org updates to share

### Agenda

#### Part 1: Their Topics (15 min)
> "What's on your mind this week?"

**Let them drive the conversation.** Common topics:
- Blockers or challenges they're facing
- Questions about project direction
- Interpersonal issues
- Concerns or anxieties
- Ideas they want to discuss

**Manager's Role:**
- Listen actively
- Ask clarifying questions
- Offer guidance or resources
- Commit to specific actions if needed

#### Part 2: Your Topics (10 min)

**Feedback on Recent Work:**
- One specific thing done well
- One area for improvement (if relevant)

**Team/Project Updates:**
- Upcoming changes or decisions
- Context they need

**Check Their Wellbeing:**
- "How are you feeling about your workload?"
- "Anything I can help unblock?"

#### Part 3: Action Items (5 min)
- [ ] Summarize key decisions
- [ ] Confirm action items (who, what, when)
- [ ] Schedule follow-ups if needed

### Post-Meeting
- [ ] Document key points and action items
- [ ] Follow up on commitments
- [ ] Share outcomes with relevant people if needed

## üöÄ Template 2: Career-Focused 1:1

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Frequency:** Monthly (or more frequent during active development)

### Pre-Meeting Prep (Manager)
- [ ] Review current development plan
- [ ] Collect evidence of recent progress
- [ ] Prepare specific examples (positive and constructive)
- [ ] Review career level expectations

### Pre-Meeting Prep (IC)
*Send these questions 24-48 hours before the meeting:*
- What progress have you made on your development goals?
- What's going well in your career growth?
- What challenges are you facing?
- What opportunities would you like to explore?

### Agenda

#### Opening (5 min)
> "Today I want to focus on your career development. I've been observing your work and collecting feedback, and I'm excited to discuss where you're at and where you're heading."

#### Part 1: Progress Review (15 min)

**Development Goals Check-in:**
- "Let's review your current development goals. How do you feel you're progressing?"
- Review each goal:
  - What evidence shows progress?
  - What's working well?
  - What's been harder than expected?
  - What support do you need?

**Recent Wins:**
- "What are you most proud of from the past month?"
- Manager adds: "I also noticed [specific example]..."

#### Part 2: Feedback & Assessment (15 min)

**Positive Feedback:**
- "Here are specific things I've observed that are going really well..."
- Use concrete examples with context
- Connect to career framework criteria

**Development Opportunities:**
- "Here are areas where I see opportunity for growth..."
- Be specific about what you're observing
- Connect to career goals or next level expectations
- Frame as opportunities, not criticism

**Their Self-Assessment:**
- "How do you assess yourself on [criterion]?"
- "Where do you feel strongest/weakest?"
- Look for alignment/misalignment with your assessment

#### Part 3: Forward Planning (15 min)

**Opportunities Ahead:**
- "Here are some projects/experiences coming up that align with your goals..."
- Discuss fit and interest

**Skill Development:**
- "What do you want to learn or improve in the next month/quarter?"
- "What resources or support would help?"

**Goal Refinement:**
- Adjust current goals if needed
- Add new goals if appropriate
- Remove goals that are no longer relevant

#### Closing (5-10 min)

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Manager will: [specific actions]
- [ ] IC will: [specific actions]
- [ ] By when: [dates]

**Next Steps:**
- When is our next career-focused 1:1?
- Any immediate needs or concerns?

### Post-Meeting
- [ ] Update development plan
- [ ] Document progress and next steps
- [ ] Follow through on commitments
- [ ] Track ongoing evidence

## üí¨ Template 3: Feedback Delivery Session

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes  
**Frequency:** As needed, or quarterly for formal feedback

### Pre-Meeting Prep (Manager)
- [ ] Prepare specific examples with full context
- [ ] Use SBI framework (Situation-Behavior-Impact)
- [ ] Balance positive and constructive
- [ ] Anticipate reactions and prepare responses
- [ ] Have concrete next steps ready

### Agenda

#### Opening (5 min)
> "I wanted to meet to share some specific feedback I have about [topic/project/behavior]. I've observed some things I want to discuss, and I'm interested in your perspective as well."

**Set the Tone:**
- We're on the same team
- Goal is growth and improvement
- This is a dialogue, not a lecture

#### Part 1: Positive Feedback (10 min)

**Use SBI Framework:**

**Situation:** "Last week in the architecture review meeting..."

**Behavior:** "...you raised a concern about the caching strategy that no one else had considered..."

**Impact:** "...which led us to catch a potential scalability issue before it reached production. Your attention to detail and willingness to speak up saved us significant future work."

**Multiple Examples:**
Share 2-3 specific positive examples

#### Part 2: Constructive Feedback (10-15 min)

**Use SBI Framework:**

**Situation:** "In the last sprint's standups..."

**Behavior:** "...I noticed you often didn't mention your blockers until the retrospective..."

**Impact:** "...which meant the team couldn't help you earlier. This resulted in the feature being delayed and you feeling more stressed than necessary."

**Ask for Their Perspective:**
- "Does this resonate with you?"
- "What was happening from your perspective?"
- "Were there factors I'm not aware of?"

**Collaborate on Solutions:**
- "What would help you raise blockers earlier?"
- "How can I support you in this?"

#### Part 3: Action Planning (10 min)

**Agree on Next Steps:**
- [ ] IC will: [specific behavior changes]
- [ ] Manager will: [specific support]
- [ ] We will: [check-in plan]

**Define Success:**
- "What would it look like if this improved?"
- "How will we know it's working?"

**Timeline:**
- When will we check in on this?
- What's a reasonable timeframe for improvement?

#### Closing (5 min)

**Summarize:**
- Key feedback points
- Agreed-upon actions
- Support available

**Check-in:**
- "How are you feeling about this conversation?"
- "What questions do you have?"
- "Is there anything else we should discuss?"

### Post-Meeting
- [ ] Document feedback given and action plan
- [ ] Send written summary if appropriate
- [ ] Schedule check-in
- [ ] Monitor for progress
- [ ] Provide ongoing coaching

## üéØ Template 4: Quarterly Goal Setting

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Frequency:** Quarterly (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)

### Pre-Meeting Prep (Manager)
- [ ] Review last quarter's goals and progress
- [ ] Identify upcoming opportunities
- [ ] Consider team/org priorities
- [ ] Review career framework expectations

### Pre-Meeting Prep (IC)
*Ask them to prepare:*
- Reflection on last quarter's goals
- Self-assessment of progress
- Ideas for next quarter
- Career aspirations/timeline

### Agenda

#### Part 1: Review Last Quarter (15 min)

**For Each Goal:**
- Did we achieve it? (Yes/No/Partial)
- What went well?
- What was challenging?
- What did you learn?

**Patterns:**
- What themes emerge?
- Which types of goals worked well?
- What do we want to do differently?

#### Part 2: Career Context (10 min)

**Big Picture:**
- "Where do you want to be in 1 year?"
- "What about 2-3 years?"
- "What experiences would be valuable?"

**Current Level Assessment:**
- How are they performing at current level?
- Any gaps to address?
- Any areas where they're exceeding?

#### Part 3: Set Next Quarter Goals (25 min)

**Goal Categories:**
1. **Technical/Engineering Craft Goals** (1-2 goals)
2. **Collaboration/Soft Skills Goals** (1-2 goals)
3. **Leadership/Impact Goals** (0-1 goals)

**For Each Goal, Define:**
- **Specific:** What exactly will they do?
- **Measurable:** How will we know it's done?
- **Achievable:** Is this realistic in one quarter?
- **Relevant:** Does it align with career path?
- **Time-bound:** When will it be complete?

**Example Goals:**

‚úÖ **Good Goal:**
"Lead the redesign of the user permissions system, including architecture proposal, stakeholder review, and implementation plan by end of Q2. Success = approved design and implementation started."

‚ùå **Vague Goal:**
"Get better at system design"

#### Part 4: Support Planning (10 min)

**For Each Goal:**
- What resources do you need?
- What projects align with this goal?
- What risks or blockers might arise?
- How can I (manager) support you?

**Manager Commitments:**
- Specific opportunities to create
- Introductions to make
- Resources to provide
- Check-in cadence

### Closing

**Document Goals:**
- [ ] Written goal statement for each
- [ ] Success criteria clear
- [ ] Support plan defined
- [ ] Check-in dates scheduled

**Next Steps:**
- Monthly check-ins on progress
- Adjust as needed (goals aren't set in stone)
- Celebrate progress along the way

### Post-Meeting
- [ ] Update official development plan
- [ ] Share with relevant stakeholders if appropriate
- [ ] Calendar monthly goal check-ins
- [ ] Start collecting evidence

## üí° General 1:1 Best Practices

### Do's ‚úÖ

**Show Up Prepared:**
- Review notes from last time
- Have specific topics ready
- Bring evidence and examples

**Listen More Than Talk:**
- 70/30 rule: They talk 70%, you talk 30%
- Ask open-ended questions
- Be comfortable with silence

**Be Present:**
- No laptop (or just for notes)
- No phone interruptions
- Close other distractions

**Take Notes:**
- Document key points
- Record action items
- Track patterns over time

**Follow Through:**
- Do what you say you'll do
- Report back on commitments
- Be reliable and consistent

### Don'ts ‚ùå

**Don't Cancel:**
- 1:1s should be sacred time
- If you must reschedule, do it proactively

**Don't Make It a Status Update:**
- This isn't standup
- Focus on development, not task lists

**Don't Skip Career Discussions:**
- At least once a month, go deep on career
- Don't let urgent push out important

**Don't Be Vague:**
- Use specific examples
- Avoid generalities
- Make feedback actionable

**Don't Hog the Conversation:**
- This is their time
- Listen more than you speak

## üéØ Customization Tips

### Adapt for Remote Teams
- Use video, not just audio
- Share screen for documents
- Use collaboration tools (Miro, Mural)
- Record notes in shared doc

### Adapt for Different Levels
- **Junior:** More frequent, more tactical
- **Senior:** Less frequent, more strategic
- **Staff+:** Collaborative, partnership model

### Adapt for Individuals
- Some want structure, some want freeform
- Some process verbally, some need prep time
- Adjust frequency based on need
- Match communication styles
