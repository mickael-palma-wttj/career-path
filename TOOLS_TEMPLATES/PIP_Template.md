## üìù Purpose of This Plan

This Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is designed to:
1. Clearly identify specific performance concerns
2. Set concrete, measurable expectations
3. Provide support and resources for improvement
4. Establish a timeline and checkpoints for progress
5. Outline consequences if improvement is not achieved

This is a formal process. We want you to succeed and are committed to supporting your improvement. However, failure to meet the expectations outlined in this plan may result in further action, up to and including termination of employment.

## üí° Tips for Creating Effective PIPs

### For Managers

**Be Specific and Objective:**
‚úÖ "PRs require 4-6 revisions; team average is 1-2"
‚ùå "Code quality is bad"

**Focus on Behaviors, Not Personality:**
‚úÖ "Misses 30% of team meetings"
‚ùå "Not committed to the team"

**Make Goals Achievable:**
- Goals should be challenging but realistic
- Provide adequate support and resources
- Consider if timeframe is reasonable

**Document Everything:**
- Keep detailed notes of all conversations
- Save emails, Slack messages, examples
- Track progress objectively
- This protects both you and the employee

**Be Consistent:**
- Apply standards equally across team
- Don't wait too long to start PIP
- Follow through on stated consequences

**Partner with HR:**
- Get HR review before issuing PIP
- Keep HR updated on progress
- Consult HR before any final decisions

---

### Common PIP Mistakes

‚ùå **Too Vague:**
"Improve performance" isn't specific enough

‚ùå **Unrealistic Goals:**
Expecting complete transformation in 30 days

‚ùå **No Support:**
Demanding improvement without providing resources

‚ùå **Not Documented:**
Starting PIP without documentation of prior issues

‚ùå **Inconsistent Application:**
Using PIP for one person but not others with similar issues

‚ùå **No HR Involvement:**
Creating PIP without HR review (legal risk!)

‚ùå **Moving Too Fast:**
Skipping informal coaching and going straight to PIP
