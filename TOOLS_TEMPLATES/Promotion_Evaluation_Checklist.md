## PHASE 1: PRE-PLANNING (3-6 Months Before)

### Understanding the Opportunity

- [ ] Understand promotion criteria for target level
- [ ] Review career level guide for target level
- [ ] Discuss with IC: "Are you interested in this level?"
- [ ] Have honest conversation: "Here's what's needed..."
- [ ] Document IC's goals and aspirations
- [ ] Create development plan with promotion focus

### Setting Expectations

- [ ] Explain what readiness looks like
- [ ] Discuss timeline (how long development typically takes)
- [ ] Clarify: "This is a development stretch, not a promise"
- [ ] Review what success indicators will be
- [ ] Address concerns or questions
- [ ] Get IC buy-in on plan

### Building Foundation

- [ ] Assign mentor or sponsor (if not manager)
- [ ] Identify development activities
- [ ] Plan stretch projects
- [ ] Coordinate with relevant stakeholders
- [ ] Begin visibility building (ensure others see the IC)
- [ ] Document baseline performance

---

## PHASE 2: ACTIVE DEVELOPMENT (3-4 Months)

### Monitoring Progress

- [ ] Regular check-ins on development plan (weekly or bi-weekly)
- [ ] Assign and track stretch projects
- [ ] Ensure mentor/sponsor relationships active
- [ ] Address blockers quickly
- [ ] Provide real-time feedback and coaching
- [ ] Document milestones and achievements

### Building Visibility

- [ ] IC leads projects/presentations visible to leadership
- [ ] IC presents at team/all-hands meetings
- [ ] Documentation of impact becomes visible
- [ ] Manager mentions IC to other leaders (positive visibility)
- [ ] Cross-team collaboration opportunities
- [ ] Create portfolio of accomplishments

### Collecting Evidence

- [ ] Gather examples of next-level capability
- [ ] Document projects and outcomes
- [ ] Collect peer feedback/testimonials
- [ ] Track quantifiable impact
- [ ] Save presentations/write-ups
- [ ] Note feedback from stakeholders

### Mid-Point Assessment

- [ ] Quick gut check: Is IC on track?
- [ ] Any concerns emerging?
- [ ] Do they seem ready? Underperforming?
- [ ] Adjust development plan if needed
- [ ] Communicate progress to IC

---

## PHASE 3: READINESS ASSESSMENT (1 Month Before)

### Self-Assessment

- [ ] Ask IC to write self-assessment
  - What's changed since starting development?
  - How do they see their readiness?
  - What are they proud of?
  - What challenges remain?
  - Why should they be promoted?

### Manager Assessment

**Capability:**
- [ ] IC demonstrates next-level technical skills
- [ ] Quality of work at expected level
- [ ] Problem-solving approach sophisticated
- [ ] Handles ambiguity and complexity
- [ ] Learning velocity strong
- [ ] Makes sound decisions

**Impact & Scope:**
- [ ] Projects have significant impact
- [ ] Scope of work at next level
- [ ] Business outcomes improved
- [ ] Cross-team/organizational influence
- [ ] Recognized for contributions
- [ ] Takes on ownership

**Readiness Signals:**
- [ ] Successfully completed stretch projects
- [ ] Positive feedback from stakeholders
- [ ] Peers see them at next level
- [ ] They feel ready (confidence without arrogance)
- [ ] Manager believes they'll succeed
- [ ] No major red flags

**Concerns to Address:**
- [ ] Any skill gaps remaining?
- [ ] Behavioral concerns?
- [ ] Relationship/collaboration issues?
- [ ] Business impact questions?
- [ ] Timeline concerns?
- [ ] Deal-breakers?

### Peer/Stakeholder Feedback

- [ ] Send 360 feedback to 6-8 peers
  - Cross-team colleagues
  - People they've led/mentored
  - Cross-functional partners
  - Direct reports (if applicable)
  - Sponsor feedback

- [ ] Questions focus on:
  - Are they ready for next level?
  - What are their strengths?
  - Areas to continue developing
  - Concerns about promotion?
  - Comparison to others at their level

- [ ] Synthesize feedback:
  - [ ] Strong consensus (ready) / Mixed / Concerns
  - [ ] Key themes that emerged
  - [ ] Any outlier feedback
  - [ ] Impact on readiness assessment

### Evidence Documentation

**Create promotion packet with:**
- [ ] Summary of responsibilities at current level
- [ ] Stretch projects completed (with outcomes)
- [ ] Quantifiable impact/metrics
- [ ] Leadership/influence examples
- [ ] Cross-team collaboration evidence
- [ ] Development/learning demonstrated
- [ ] Peer feedback themes
- [ ] Mentor/sponsor observations
- [ ] Comparison to others at current vs. next level

---

## PHASE 4: CALIBRATION & DECISION (2 Weeks Before)

### Internal Calibration

- [ ] Discuss with manager peer group
  - Does this IC compare well to others promoted?
  - Are promotion criteria consistently applied?
  - Any concerns from others?
  - Any information we're missing?

- [ ] Consult with:
  - [ ] Skip-level manager
  - [ ] HR/People partner
  - [ ] Mentor/sponsor
  - [ ] Other promotion calibrators
  - [ ] IC's sponsor (if different from manager)

### Addressing Concerns

**If concerns exist:**
- [ ] Document the concerns specifically
- [ ] Is this a deal-breaker or development area?
- [ ] Can concerns be addressed before promotion?
- [ ] Do we promote and support, or wait?
- [ ] What's the IC's perspective?

**Decision tree:**
- No concerns + strong evidence = GO
- Minor concerns + strong fundamentals = GO with support plan
- Major concerns + fundamental gaps = NO GO
- Unclear = Gather more info, extend timeline

### Making the Decision

**Go Decision:**
- [ ] All criteria met
- [ ] Evidence strong
- [ ] Consensus among stakeholders
- [ ] IC ready and willing
- [ ] Business timing right
- [ ] Plan support/onboarding in new level

**No Go Decision:**
- [ ] Documented reason(s)
- [ ] Specific gaps identified
- [ ] Plan for next opportunity
- [ ] Development areas clear
- [ ] Timeline for next review
- [ ] Support to close gaps

**Conditional Go Decision:**
- [ ] Specific conditions identified
- [ ] Timeline to address
- [ ] Support plan clear
- [ ] Review date set
- [ ] IC agreement on path

---

## PHASE 5: COMMUNICATION (1 Week Before)

### Preparing the IC

**If Go Decision:**
- [ ] Tell IC first (before any announcements)
- [ ] In person if possible
- [ ] Congratulate them
- [ ] Explain the decision (what convinced us)
- [ ] Discuss start date
- [ ] Explain what happens next
- [ ] Answer questions

**If No Go Decision:**
- [ ] Tell IC in private conversation
- [ ] Be direct and compassionate
- [ ] Explain specific gaps
- [ ] Don't sugarcoat but be kind
- [ ] Discuss next steps
- [ ] Create plan to address gaps
- [ ] Set timeline for next review
- [ ] Affirm continued growth

### Communicating to Team

- [ ] Announce to IC's team
- [ ] Congratulate IC and team
- [ ] Explain impact on team
- [ ] Discuss backfill plan
- [ ] Support team transition

- [ ] Announce to broader organization
  - All-hands announcement
  - Internal communications
  - Update org chart/systems
  - Social media/announcements

### Communicating to Stakeholders

- [ ] Notify mentors/sponsors
- [ ] Tell feedback providers
- [ ] Update HR/People systems
- [ ] Update promotion records
- [ ] Notify cross-functional partners

---

## PHASE 6: ONBOARDING TO NEW LEVEL (First Month)

### First Week

- [ ] Welcome to new level
- [ ] Discuss first priorities
- [ ] Introduce to new peer group
- [ ] Explain expectations
- [ ] Set first month goals
- [ ] Clear any initial questions
- [ ] Reduce old level responsibilities

- [ ] Check in on feelings:
  - How are they feeling?
  - Any surprises?
  - Anything unexpected?
  - Imposter syndrome normal—normalize it

### First Month

- [ ] Weekly 1:1s (increased from usual)
- [ ] More frequent check-ins
- [ ] Available for questions/coaching
- [ ] Celebrate early wins
- [ ] Address concerns quickly
- [ ] Introduce to new relationships
- [ ] Set realistic expectations

**Expect:**
- [ ] Learning curve (steeper than expected)
- [ ] Moments of doubt ("Am I ready?")
- [ ] Slower progress initially
- [ ] Some failures or misses
- [ ] Time to figure out how to operate

**Provide:**
- [ ] Patient coaching
- [ ] Clear expectations
- [ ] Permission to not be perfect
- [ ] Access to mentors
- [ ] Regular feedback and support

### First Quarter

- [ ] Monthly check-ins with manager
- [ ] 360 check-in at 3 months
- [ ] Early wins celebrated
- [ ] Areas needing development identified
- [ ] Continued mentorship
- [ ] Integration into peer group
- [ ] Course corrections made quickly

## EVALUATION CRITERIA REFERENCE

### For Each Level, Typical Readiness Indicators

**Confirmed → Senior:**
- [ ] Consistently exceeds expectations at current level
- [ ] Technical depth in key areas
- [ ] Shows independent judgment
- [ ] Takes on leadership informally
- [ ] Mentors or helps junior ICs
- [ ] Contributes beyond immediate team
- [ ] Receives positive feedback consistently

**Senior → Staff:**
- [ ] Recognized as technical expert
- [ ] Leads major technical initiatives
- [ ] Influential across team/org
- [ ] Mentors multiple people
- [ ] Makes strategic technical decisions
- [ ] Drives architectural thinking
- [ ] Cross-functional collaboration strong

**Staff → Senior Staff:**
- [ ] Company-wide technical influence
- [ ] Shapes technical strategy
- [ ] Mentors other staff engineers
- [ ] Recognized expert in critical areas
- [ ] Executive presence
- [ ] Drives organizational capability
- [ ] External visibility/leadership

---

## RED FLAGS (When to Say No or Delay)

❌ **Deal-breakers (usually means no promotion):**
- Integrity concerns
- Serious behavioral issues
- Harassment or discrimination
- Ethical violations
- Consistent underperformance

❌ **Major concerns (usually means delay):**
- Skill gaps in critical area
- Unresolved conflict with key stakeholders
- Business impact falling short
- IC not ready (despite capability)
- Timing wrong for organization

⚠️ **Minor concerns (can promote with support plan):**
- One skill area needs development
- Some peer feedback concerns
- Area needs coaching/support
- Some visibility gaps
- Communication could improve
