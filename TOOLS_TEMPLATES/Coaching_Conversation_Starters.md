# Coaching Conversation Starters

Opening prompts and conversation frameworks for coaching IC development across common topics and scenarios.

---

## Using This Resource

**When to use:**
- Starting difficult or sensitive conversations
- Coaching on development areas
- Working through challenges
- Career conversations
- Performance feedback discussions
- Problem-solving conversations

**How to use:**
1. Choose the topic that matches your situation
2. Pick an opening question that resonates
3. Listen more than talk
4. Ask follow-up questions
5. Help them find their own solutions
6. Document insights for next check-in

**Tip:** The best coaching questions make people think, not just answer.

---

## CAREER & GROWTH CONVERSATIONS

### When IC is Considering Next Level

**Opening question:**
"I've noticed you're asking good questions about the next level. What's got you thinking about growth?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What appeals to you about the next level?"
- "What concerns do you have?"
- "What do you think is holding you back?"
- "What would make you feel ready?"
- "How are you currently preparing?"

**Where to go:**
- Create development plan
- Identify gaps to close
- Plan stretch projects
- Ensure realistic timeline

---

### When IC is Unclear About Career Direction

**Opening question:**
"I sense you might be thinking about your career differently. What's on your mind?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What was energizing you before?"
- "What's changed?"
- "If you could do anything here, what would it be?"
- "What would make you excited about coming to work?"
- "What are you curious about?"

**Where to go:**
- Explore different paths (IC leadership, management, specialization)
- Consider what matters most to them
- Discuss opportunities available
- Align work with interests

---

### When IC Wants Management Track

**Opening question:**
"You've mentioned interest in management. Tell me more about what appeals to you."

**Follow-ups:**
- "What do you think management involves?"
- "Have you managed before or led teams?"
- "What about IC leadership track? Does that interest you?"
- "What would success look like?"
- "What concerns do you have?"

**Where to go:**
- Test interest (stretch project with leadership aspects)
- Clarify why (ambition vs. running away)
- Explore IC leadership alternatives
- Create honest path forward

## SKILL & CAPABILITY DEVELOPMENT

### When IC is Struggling with Technical Skill

**Opening question:**
"I've noticed you're working through [technical area]. How is that going for you?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What's the hardest part?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What resources do you have available?"
- "Who else has done this well? Could you learn from them?"
- "What would help you accelerate?"

**Where to go:**
- Identify knowledge gaps
- Connect to expert/mentor
- Break into smaller pieces
- Create learning plan
- Regular check-ins on progress

---

### When IC is Stuck on a Problem

**Opening question:**
"You've been working on this for a while. What are you thinking about?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me what you've tried so far."
- "What happened when you tried that?"
- "What's blocking you from moving forward?"
- "If you had to guess, what's the root cause?"
- "What would the solution look like?"

**Where to go:**
- Help them think through systematically
- Break into smaller pieces
- Identify what they know vs. don't
- Plan next steps
- Escalate if needed

---

### When IC Needs to Learn Something New

**Opening question:**
"We're going to need [new skill] on our team. How would you feel about being the person who drives that?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What would you need to be successful?"
- "How would you approach learning this?"
- "Who else has expertise we can learn from?"
- "What's your timeline?"
- "How can I support you?"

**Where to go:**
- Create learning plan
- Allocate time for learning
- Connect to resources/experts
- Track progress
- Recognize when competent

## COLLABORATION & COMMUNICATION

### When IC Struggles with a Peer

**Opening question:**
"I've noticed some tension with [peer]. How are things going there?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me your perspective on what happened."
- "How do you think they see it?"
- "What would help resolve this?"
- "What role did you play?"
- "What would you do differently?"

**Where to go:**
- Understand both perspectives
- Help see other's viewpoint
- Coach on communication
- Plan conversation with peer (if appropriate)
- Follow up on resolution

---

### When IC Needs Better Communication

**Opening question:**
"I want to give you some feedback on how we communicate. Can we talk about that?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "In our last meeting, I noticed [specific behavior]."
- "What do you think was happening?"
- "How did it land for you?"
- "What would be more effective?"
- "Let's practice that..."

**Where to go:**
- Get IC's perspective
- Help them see impact
- Practice new approach
- Role-play if helpful
- Check in on progress

---

### When IC Doesn't Seek Help When Needed

**Opening question:**
"I've noticed you sometimes get stuck before reaching out. Can we talk about that?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What makes you hesitant to ask for help?"
- "Are you worried about looking bad?"
- "What if you got help earlier?"
- "Who could you reach out to?"
- "What would make it easier to ask?"

**Where to go:**
- Normalize asking for help
- Build psychological safety
- Clarify when to ask vs. solve
- Lower threshold for asking
- Celebrate when they ask

## FEEDBACK & PERFORMANCE

### When Giving Difficult Feedback

**Opening question:**
"I want to share some feedback with you. Can we talk about [situation]?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Here's what I observed: [specific behavior]"
- "The impact was: [specific result]"
- "I'm sharing this because: [because I care about you / it matters for your growth]"
- "What are you thinking?"
- "What would you do differently?"

**Where to go:**
- State clearly, don't be vague
- Focus on impact, not judgment
- Help them see it
- Agree on what changes
- Check in on progress

---

### When Giving Positive Feedback

**Opening question:**
"I wanted to give you some feedback. You did something really well."

**Follow-ups:**
- "Here's what I noticed: [specific action]"
- "The impact was: [specific result]"
- "I wanted you to know: [why this matters]"
- "More of this, please!"
- "How can we build on this?"

**Where to go:**
- Be specific and genuine
- Help them see their impact
- Encourage more of it
- Connect to bigger picture
- Reinforce the behavior

---

### When IC Gets Defensive About Feedback

**Opening question:**
"I sense this feedback landed hard. What are you thinking?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Help me understand your reaction."
- "What are you disagreeing with?"
- "What part resonates?"
- "Let's separate disagreement from learning."
- "What would help you hear this?"

**Where to go:**
- Create space for reaction
- Don't push back on defensiveness
- Listen to their perspective
- Find what might be true
- Agree to revisit if needed

## DIFFICULT SITUATIONS

### When IC Made a Mistake

**Opening question:**
"Let's talk about what happened with [situation]. Walk me through it."

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me your perspective."
- "What were you thinking at the time?"
- "What would you do differently?"
- "What did you learn?"
- "How do we prevent this?"

**Where to go:**
- Understand what happened
- Help them see lesson
- Don't dwell on blame
- Focus on learning
- Plan prevention

---

### When IC is Frustrated

**Opening question:**
"I sense some frustration. What's going on?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me what's frustrating you."
- "Why does this matter to you?"
- "What would help?"
- "What's in your control?"
- "What can we do together?"

**Where to go:**
- Validate feelings
- Understand root cause
- Focus on what you can change
- Brainstorm solutions
- Take action

---

### When IC is Considering Leaving

**Opening question:**
"I've gotten the sense you might be thinking about what's next. What's going on?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me what's making you think about this."
- "What would make you want to stay?"
- "What's not working?"
- "Is this about the role, team, company?"
- "Is there anything I can do?"

**Where to go:**
- Listen, don't try to change mind
- Understand true concerns
- Fix what you can fix
- Be honest if you can't
- Respect their decision

## GROWTH CONVERSATION FLOW

### Generic Coaching Conversation (Any Topic)

**1. Opening (Get on same page)**
- "I want to talk about [topic]."
- "How are you feeling about [situation]?"

**2. Understanding (Listen first)**
- "Tell me what's going on from your perspective."
- "What's the challenge you're facing?"
- "What have you already tried?"

**3. Reflection (Help them think)**
- "What do you think is happening?"
- "How does that connect to [bigger picture]?"
- "What would you do if you had no constraints?"

**4. Brainstorm (Explore options)**
- "What are some possible approaches?"
- "What haven't we considered?"
- "Who could help?"

**5. Plan (Decide together)**
- "What feels right to try first?"
- "What's your commitment?"
- "How will we know if it's working?"

**6. Close (Agree on next step)**
- "Let's check in [timeframe]."
- "I'll [what I'll do]."
- "You'll [what they'll do]."

## COACHING PRINCIPLES

**Best practices for all conversations:**

✓ **Listen more than talk** - Let them find the answer
✓ **Ask questions** - Not statements
✓ **Assume positive intent** - Start there
✓ **Separate person from behavior** - Criticize behavior, not them
✓ **Be specific** - Give examples, not generalizations
✓ **Focus on impact** - What happened because of this?
✓ **Build psychological safety** - Make it safe to be honest
✓ **Follow up** - Check on progress, don't abandon
✓ **Document** - Note what you discussed
✓ **Iterate** - Adjust approach based on response
