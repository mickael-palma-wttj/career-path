# Interview Questions By Level

Calibrated interview questions for assessing IC capability at each career level, useful for hiring, promotion evaluation, and skill assessment.

---

## Using These Questions

**When to use:**
- Hiring at each level
- Promotion interviews
- Skip-level conversations to assess capability
- Skill assessment interviews
- Career development interviews

**How to use:**
- Pick 3-4 questions per interview (don't ask all)
- Listen for depth, not just "right answer"
- Ask follow-ups: "Tell me more...", "How did you..."
- Take notes on specific examples
- Compare answers to find patterns
- Calibrate across candidates

**Tip:** Look for evidence of the capability, not canned answers.

## LEVEL 1: JUNIOR DEVELOPER

### Technical Foundation Questions

**Q1.1: Learning Approach**
"Tell me about a time you had to learn something new on the job. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Proactive learning mindset
- Asking for help appropriately
- Using available resources
- Persistence despite not knowing
- Comfort with not knowing

**Q1.2: Problem-Solving**
"Describe a bug or issue you worked on. Walk me through how you debugged it."

*What we're listening for:*
- Systematic thinking
- Asking good questions
- Using tools/debugging approaches
- When they ask for help
- How they learn from the process

**Q1.3: Code Quality**
"How do you think about code quality? What makes code 'good'?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Awareness of best practices
- Readability/maintainability thinking
- Understanding of tradeoffs
- Learning from code reviews
- Care about craft

### Collaboration Questions

**Q1.4: Receiving Feedback**
"Tell me about feedback you received that was hard to hear. How did you respond?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Receptivity to feedback
- Growth mindset
- Humility
- Ability to implement change
- Resilience

**Q1.5: Team Contribution**
"Describe a time you helped someone else on your team. What did you do?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Willingness to help
- Understanding of team dynamic
- When they jump in vs. wait
- Generosity with knowledge
- Humility in helping

### Motivation Questions

**Q1.6: Growth Mindset**
"What's something you want to get better at? How are you working on it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Self-awareness about gaps
- Proactive development
- Specific growth goals
- Realistic timeline
- Motivation to improve

## LEVEL 2: CONFIRMED DEVELOPER

### Technical Depth Questions

**Q2.1: Complex Problem**
"Tell me about a complex technical problem you solved. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Breaking down complex problems
- Multiple approaches considered
- Tradeoffs evaluated
- Architectural thinking
- Learning from outcome

**Q2.2: System Understanding**
"Describe a system you know well. Walk me through its architecture and why it's designed that way."

*What we're listening for:*
- Deep technical knowledge
- Understanding design decisions
- Big picture thinking
- Ability to explain clearly
- Awareness of tradeoffs

**Q2.3: Technical Leadership**
"Tell me about a technical decision you influenced or led. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Taking initiative
- Thinking through tradeoffs
- Bringing others along
- Making data-driven decisions
- Considering impact

### Impact & Influence Questions

**Q2.4: Project Impact**
"Tell me about a project you led or significantly contributed to. What was the impact?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Ownership and drive
- Quantifiable impact
- Cross-team collaboration
- Problem-solving approach
- How they measure success

**Q2.5: Influence Without Authority**
"Describe a time you influenced a decision without having direct authority. How did you do it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Persuasion skills
- Building consensus
- Handling disagreement
- Focus on outcomes not control
- Relationship-building

### Development & Mentorship

**Q2.6: Helping Others Grow**
"Tell me about helping someone develop or learn something. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Mentorship instincts
- Explaining clearly
- Patience with others
- Building confidence
- Follow-through

**Q2.7: Career Perspective**
"What are you working toward next in your career? How are you preparing?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Career thinking
- Self-awareness about next level
- Proactive development
- Realistic assessment
- Ambition and drive

## LEVEL 4: STAFF DEVELOPER

### Strategic & Organizational Questions

**Q4.1: Multi-Year Vision**
"Tell me about defining or influencing a multi-year technical vision or strategy. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Strategic thinking (2-3+ years)
- Anticipating future needs
- Positioning org for growth
- Considering multiple scenarios
- Long-term impact

**Q4.2: Cross-Org Complexity**
"Tell me about navigating a complex, org-wide challenge. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Org-wide perspective
- Stakeholder complexity management
- Political awareness without being political
- Building alignment
- Driving change at scale

**Q4.3: Technical Transformation**
"Tell me about leading a significant technical transformation or capability build. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Change leadership
- Handling resistance
- Bringing teams along
- Measuring progress
- Adaptability

### Industry & External Questions

**Q4.4: External Contribution**
"Tell me about contributing to the industry or community (conference talks, open source, etc.). How did that impact your thinking?"

*What we're listening for:*
- External perspective
- Thought leadership
- Continuous learning
- Giving back
- Industry awareness

**Q4.5: Emerging Trends**
"What emerging trends do you see in technology? How do you think about them?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Forward thinking
- Pattern recognition
- Discernment (not reactive)
- How this affects their org
- Continuous learning

**Q4.6: Mentoring Staff Engineers**
"Tell me about mentoring or developing other senior/staff engineers. How have you approached it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Developing peer-level engineers
- Sharing experience not just direction
- Creating psychological safety
- Expanding peer's perspective
- Sustainability

### Judgment & Decision-Making Questions

**Q4.7: High-Stakes Decision**
"Tell me about a high-stakes technical or organizational decision you were involved in. How did you approach it?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Handling ambiguity and uncertainty
- Considering multiple perspectives
- Managing risk
- Using data AND judgment
- Learning from outcomes

**Q4.8: When You Were Wrong**
"Tell me about a significant decision where you were wrong. What did you learn?"

*What we're listening for:*
- Humility and self-awareness
- Honest reflection
- Learning orientation
- How that changed approach
- Maturity

## CALIBRATION ACROSS LEVELS

### What We're Looking For

| Level | Technical Depth | Scope | Influence | Strategic Thinking |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Junior** | Single area | Own code | Team | Current sprint |
| **Confirmed** | Multiple areas | Project/feature | Team + adjacent | Current quarter |
| **Senior** | Deep in areas | Team/system | Org/cross-team | 6-12 months |
| **Staff** | Broad, multiple deep | Org-wide | Organization | 1-2 years |
| **Sr Staff** | Industry perspective | Company | Executive, industry | 2-3 years |

### Red Flags Across Levels

**Junior:**
- ❌ Not asking questions
- ❌ Defensive when getting feedback
- ❌ Blaming others for issues
- ❌ Not learning from mistakes

**Confirmed:**
- ❌ Isolated problem-solver (doesn't collaborate)
- ❌ Doesn't take on challenges beyond comfort zone
- ❌ Dismissive of others' ideas
- ❌ Not developing judgment

**Senior:**
- ❌ Thinks only about own work (no org perspective)
- ❌ Doesn't mentor others
- ❌ Can't influence without authority
- ❌ Limited to one technology area

**Staff:**
- ❌ Only thinks tactically (not strategic)
- ❌ Doesn't develop other staff engineers
- ❌ Limited external awareness
- ❌ Doesn't scale impact

**Sr Staff:**
- ❌ Not thinking at org/board level
- ❌ No thought leadership visible
- ❌ Doesn't shape industry thinking
- ❌ Limited executive presence

## SCORING GUIDE

**Use this to assess answers:**

| Score | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Well below level | Generic answer, no depth, little evidence |
| **2** | Below level | Some evidence, but limited depth or perspective |
| **3** | At level | Clear evidence of capability at this level |
| **4** | Above level | Strong evidence, exceptional thinking |
| **5** | Well above level | Exceptional for this level, ready for next |
